Chen Li

Assistant Professor · National University of Defense Technology · lichen@nudt.edu.cn

I am an assistant professor in the college of computer at National University of Defense Technology (NUDT). My research interests are in the GPU Architecture, GPU Unified Virtual Memory and Network-on-Chips.

I got my Ph.D, M.E and B.E degrees from NUDT, advised by Professor Yang Guo. During my Ph.D., I visited Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at University of Pittsburgh, co-advised by Prof. Jun Yang and Prof. Youtao Zhang .

Research Experience

Research on GPU Unified Virtual Memory

  • Investigating the source and behaviour of GPU oversubscription
  • Proposed a framework for memory oversubscription management
  • Recovered 75% of performance loss due to oversubscription
    • Oct. 2017 - Present

    Research on GPU Preemption

  • Investigated previous GPU pre-emption methods
  • Proposed a dynamic and proactive GPU pre-emption mechanism using checkpointing scheme
  • Achieved 58.6% of stall time reduction for context switching
    • Oct. 2016 - Sep. 2017

    Research on Three-Dimensional Networks-on-Chip

  • Surveyed the current research on 3D NoC and 3D IC products
  • Studied the interposer-based 2.5D NoC
  • Proposed a latency-aware load-balanced strategy in 2.5D NoC architecture
  • Achieved 69.4% performance improvement compared to the dateline router
    • Jan. 2015 - Sep. 2016

    Research on Multi-layer and non-blocking ring and hierarchical ring

  • Designed a multi-layer and non-blocking ring
  • Our ring obtained performance gains compared with three kinds of rings
  • Focused on hundred-core processor, proposed a hybrid hierarchical ring
    • Nov. 2013 - Dec. 2014

    The Project of FT high performance DSP

  • Participated in the front-end logic design and implementation
  • Finished the Timer module and inter-core communication module
    • Dec. 2012 - Dec. 2013
